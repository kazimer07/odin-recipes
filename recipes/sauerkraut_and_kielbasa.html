Sauerkraut and Kielbasa

This is a recipe that combines the parts of both of my grandmothers recipes with some of my own flairs. My maternal grandmother's recipe is very Polish or so I'm told whereas my paternal grandmother's recipe is more German with Hungarian elements. Regardless, my recipe contains a lot of garlic, onion, and paprika.

Ingredients

  • 2lbs. Smoked Kielbasa
  • 1 Jar Polish Style Sauerkraut
  • 1 Large Yellow Onion Diced
  • 4 Garlic Cloves Minced
  • 2 Thick Cut Bacon Slices
  • 2 Tablespoons Paprika
  • 2 Bay Leaves

Equipment

  • Skillet
  • Knife
  • Cutting Board
  • Crockpot

Procedure

  1. Drain and Rinse your sauerkraut. Set aside while allowing to drain.
  2. Cut kielbasa into rounds of your desired thickness. (I prefer mine about a 1/4" thick.)
  3. Place bacon into skillet and turn on medium low heat.
  4. Once cripsy, remove bacon and set aside.
  5. Add diced onion to skillet and sautee.
  6. Add garlic when onions are translucent and continue sauteeing.
  7. Once fully cooked add onions and garlic to Crockpot.
  8. Lightly brown kielbasa in skillet.
  9. Add kielbasa, sauerkraut, bay leaves, paprika, and just enough water to cover.
  10. Mix well and set Crockpot to low.
  11. Optional: Empty contents of Crockpot into a large skillet and cook to remove excess moisture.
